BEST KUNAFA RECIPE FROM SCRATH

If you want something to satisfy your sweet tooth, you must try this Kunafa recipe. It is an iconic, devilishly delicious Middle Eastern dessert, made with shredded Kataifi, creamy filling and soaked in condensed milk.

WHAT IS KUNAFE?

So its time to check on some traditional recipes. Have you ever felt the incredible taste of cream in some traditional recipes? Well, the Kunafa recipe should be your starting point. The Kunafa, also known as Kanafeh or Knafeh, is a popular dessert in the Middle East and commonly eaten in the Arab world. It is popularly consumed during the Muslim Ramadan period. However, it is a nice dessert one can consume at any time of the year.

It comes in different shapes and fillings (cheese, chocolate, fruits etc) to satisfy your taste bud. The orange-coloured dessert is not only exceptional in appearance, but also in its tangy, sweet taste. The yummy and nutritious delicacy is even a source of income for some individuals. The Kunafa is prepared by stuffing cream , milk and other dairy products into two layers of shredded phyllo (kataifi) and subsequently baking it. It is best served hot. Just like pizzas, it is often prepared in big rounds and then cut into sizes so everyone can have a bite.

INGREDIENTS FOR KUNAFA RECIPE

Quite alright, the Kunafa recipe contains many ingredients but its certainly worth a try.

  • Milk
  • Heavy cream
  • Condensed milk
  • Kunafa dough
  • Sugar
  • Cornstarch
  • Butter
  • Pistachio powder

You can garnish the dessert with pistachios, cinnamon or powdered sugar, and enjoy it with tea. The Kunafa is a highly nutritious dessert.

HOW TO STORE KUNAFA LEFTOVERS

Handling leftovers: In case of any leftover, simply transfer them to an airtight vessel and keep in your refrigerator. It should last for a week in there. When you are ready to consume your dessert, simply reheat in your oven at a temperature of 350 F for about 15 mins.

KUNAFA   PLATE

Kunafa is baked in a Kunafa plate traditionally, however even a cake pan will work.

BEST KUNAFA RECIPE

KUNAFA RECIPE

  • Author: Chefjar
  • Prep Time: 15 mins
  • Cook Time: 30 mins
  • Total Time: 45 mins
  • Yield: 4-6
Print Recipe
Pin Recipe

Description

If you want something to satisfy your sweet tooth, you must try this Kunafa recipe. It is an iconic, devilishly delicious Middle Eastern dessert, made with shredded Kataifi, creamy filling and soaked in condensed milk.

Ingredients

  • FOR THE SYRUP
  • Condensed milk- 1/2 can ( 400 gm)
  • FOR THE KUNAFA FILLING
  • Full fat milk-3/4 cup
  • Heavy cream- 1 3/4 cup
  • Sugar- 1 1/2 tbsp.
  • Cornstarch -2 tbsp.
  • FOR KUNAFA CRUST
  • Semi-frozen Kunafa dough( shredded phyllo pastry)- 250 gm or 0.5 lbs
  • Unsalted butter/ Ghee( melted, room temperature)-1/2 cup

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 180 C/ 356 F with the rack on the bottom of the oven.
  2. Kunafa crust. Coat your 22 cm/ 9 inch baking tray with some butter/ghee.
  3. Shred semi-frozen kunafa dough into 1.5 inch/ 4 cm long pieces over a large bowl. Add the remaining butter and mix with kunafa shreds to coat evenly. Transfer half of the dough to the tray and press firmly ,lining sides of the tray so it will hold the cream filling and prevent it from burning.
  5. Making cream filling. In a medium saucepan heat milk and heavy cream; then add sugar and cornstarch and whisk until completely dissolved over medium heat. Bring to a boil, stirring constantly. Boil until the mixture thickens. Remove from the stove and pour over the crust, spread with a spatula.
  6. Top with the remaining kunafa shreds evenly and bake 30-40 minutes or until golden.
  8. Remove kunafa from the oven and pour condensed milk, covering the entire surface. You might not need the whole can.
  9. Garnish with ground pistachio.
  10. Allow kunafa rest for 10-15 min to absorb the condensed milk.
  11. Serve warm.

Kunafeh plate

Notes

EQUIPMENT:
A 9 inch/ 22 cm round baking dish.

MAKING AHEAD OF TIME
You can assemble kunafa in a baking dish, cover with plastic and freeze. Thaw, once you are ready to bake. Bake as mentioned n the recipe above.

DEEP ORANGE COLOR
To give kunafa deep orange color, you will need to mix orange food coloring with the melted butter and pour it over the shredded phyllo dough; then mix using latex gloves to prevent hands from coloring.
I didnt have orange color on hand, I used liquid red color and yellow to get orange.

THICKER FILLING
If you prefer thicker filling, use milk-1 cup, heavy cream-1 cup and 3 tbsp. of cornstarch.

WHERE TO BUY KUNAFA/KADAIFI DOUGH
You can find kadaifi dough in Middle Eastern stores, amazon or in online Middle Eastern Food Stores.
